## Context

What problem are we solving? What forces are at play (technical,
organisational, scale, deadline, cost)? Why is this decision being
taken now and not earlier or later?

State the situation in enough detail that a reader who lands here in
two years can reconstruct the constraints without external references.

## Decision

The decision in one or two sentences. State it in present tense ("We
use trunk-based branching with `main` as the only long-lived
branch"), not future tense ("We will use…").

Follow with the supporting details: the specific shape of the choice,
any thresholds or quantities, and the boundary cases it covers.

## Consequences

What follows from this decision — both intended and unintended.

**Positive**: what this enables / unblocks.

**Negative**: what this costs us, what becomes harder, what we now
have to maintain that we did not before.

**Neutral**: changes in shape that are not obviously good or bad.

## Alternatives considered

For each alternative we ruled out, state:

- The alternative in one line.
- The reason we did not pick it.

Two or three is plenty; the goal is to demonstrate we considered the
problem, not to enumerate every possible option.
